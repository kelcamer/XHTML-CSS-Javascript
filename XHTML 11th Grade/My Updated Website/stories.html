Florian's Stories

My boyfriend's name is Florian Gawlitta. He loves to write, and he wants to write as many books as he can. Writing is his passion, and he wants to know what you guys think about his stories. :)

 

       "A tree is nothing without the leaves. And the leaves are nothing without the tree. That's how it has always been, and that's how nature created it." He didn't mind that he talked to himself as he looked out of the slightly open window. The cherry trees outside were blooming and the wind was gently blowing his hair behind his face - another force of nature, he thought, that can be so nice when weak, but devastating when strong. He rested his elbows on the windowsill and watched the wind blowing off several cherry blossom leaves.


      "If everything could be so simple as nature", he said, looking away with disgust, not of nature, but of humans who relentlessly kill each other over things like money or honour. "I wish I'd be a leaf", he said to himself, looking at the spectacle in front of his eyes. The wind blew the leaves on a nearby house, and when they hit the ground, they rested, knowing their journey has come to an end. "That's how leaves are", he silently muttered. "Their whole life they were attached to one tree, they grew up with it. They lived with and depended on each other. The leaf can't live without the tree, or else it dies. And a tree without leaves..."


       He stopped, grabbing a cigarette from a nearby table and lit it. As he blew the smoke outside the window, it joined together with the wind, carrying it away to the sky. He smiled. "A tree without leaves is useless. The leaves are doing all the work. They are producing the air we need to breathe. The tree is merely the holder." The cigarette was growing shorter in his shaking hands, and he clumsily scratched the back of his neck. "Like a human", he said in a mocking tone, moving around his arms.       


 

"Without the body, my arms would be useless. My arms are like leaves. But without the arms, my body would turn useless too." He stopped for a moment, and smiled. "Well, at least it would hinder me a lot. Like a tree." He was finishing the cigarette and looked out of the window again. The wind was getting stronger, and his eyes glanced thoughtfully over the cherry trees. "A lot of air will fade tonight", he said, observing the intensity of the upcoming storm. "And a lot of leaves are going to lose their life. A brutality." He sighed. He was getting melancholy more often lately, but he always felt it was for a good reason. On days like this, were the storm was brewing and everyone was trying to stay inside, he thought of all the poor animals and plants. He laughed at the irony.




      Humans always say they are the most adaptable species on the planet. And still, as they evolved, he felt they were getting weaker. When it rained, they searched shelter instead of enduring it. Any hardships that they had to face they tried to avoid rather than to overcome. The thing is that he couldn't tell if this was a sign of intelligence or degeneration. He coughed, the smoke hanging in his throat.




       Looking at the cigarette stump though he realized that the humans were slowly destroying themselves anyway. He was no exception. "Maybe humans who formed habits can't break out of them anymore", he whispered, as if afraid someone could hear him. "Maybe...the leaves sometimes want to leave the tree. If it becomes corrupt, if it becomes soulless, and if it becomes caught in it's own habit..." He gasped. "Or diseased." He looked down at the cigarette again, then his arms and legs. "I can make my own decisions. These leaves can't. As sad as this is, I am a human." He smiled, looking out in the darkness. The moon was now shining and illuminating the place in an almost eerie fashion. "Seems I need to make the most out of what I am", he said, awaiting the new day. Because humans shouldn't be thrown around in the wind like leaves."

 

 




      "The storms were raging,  waves crashed upon the boat, the rain was falling hard and heavy, trying to enter their minds with its beating sound, but they didn't move or collapse. They knew their goal, focused on their only need, they kept moving forward, no matter what difficulties did rise upon them. They did need neither fortune nor luck, rather they kept working towards their goals they wanted to reach. They controlled their power inside their heads, their thoughts being channeled into a hexagon, each corner of it being one part of the passion they poured into their collective mission.



      They looked up on their small boat, it being nothing more than their tool that should have taken them where they wanted to be. Now out on wide sea, their eyes turned to slits, their hexagons tightened, holding onto the last sliver of hope they had left, as the beast of the sea threw wave upon wave towards them. The boat was shaking, the grip of the ocean wrapped tightly around it, like a men holds his love, just that this grip was more violently than anything they've ever felt. They knew they needed to remain calm, to focus, to pour all their hope, love, passion and dedication into getting this boat to the island they wanted to get to.




      Then suddenly, the sun rose slowly, covering part of the sky in an orange that looked so innocent, pure and lovely as a newborn, giving the perfect contrast to the raging tide underneath their feet.


      Their goals were all different yet the same - one wanted to be united with the love of his life, living on the very island he wanted to reach no matter the cost - another wanted to begin a new life on a place that is rich with possibilities. Yet another wanted to make as much money as he could on this island - but they all agreed that the most noble goal belonged to the person who wanted to be with the person he loved.


      He always sat in the corner of the boat, never talking much - and even now, that the boat was shaking and the sea was ravaging, he seemed calm, as if the essence and core of his mind would have been channeled into only the thought of the person he wanted to be with more than anything. His green eyes clashed with the dark blue sea, yet he kept talking of how much the colour of the sea reminded him of her eyes. He seemed happy even now that their lives seemed in grave danger. Still, he was giving directions to everyone on the ship despite not being the captain, taking everything as easily as a morning walk in the park - it inspired everyone around him, and they secured the ship.




      Even the captain seemed more secure as he steered it around the waves, his grip tight around the sterring wheel. It felt like a ride in a demonic rollercoaster, like destiny playing the cards, like Fortuna rolling the dice - and everyone was hoping that she wouldn't roll a 6. But everyone knew it would be alright the moment the sea was calming down and the sun was rising up the sky farther and farther, changing the orange more and more into a bright blue. The hexagons that have tightened in their mind released with a creeking sound, and the silver lining became clear.




       The boy with the green eyes ran to the front of the boat, and started crying. The other members of the crew followed hastily and saw what made him cry - the island was visible, a glorious sight. The boats horn was telling it of their arrival.




      Dozens of people ran towards the shore and greeted them with waves, wondering about the strangers, curiosity in their faces. And amongst these people was a girl, with long hair, blue eyes, even bluer than the ocean.


       She ran into the ocean, swimming in the direction of the ship. The boy knew it was the girl he wanted to see, the girl he conquered the sea and braced the storm for. He jumped into the water and swam towards her, and everyone was looking at them, astounded by their love for each other. It emanated from their hearts, colouring the sea red and the sky purple. They hugged, kissed, cried, and even as the boat finally hit the shore, they were still swimming around, happy they finally had each other. They soon left the island to start their life together, and no one ever saw them again after that day - but they all knew they found happiness somewhere, being together.


      The ship has reached its goal, the members were happy. Everyone found what they sought.

A perfect journey did unfold."
